Definition

What is the Range?

The Range

The range is the difference between the highest value and the lowest value in a dataset.

It tells us how spread out — or how consistent — a set of data is.

Range = Max − Min

A large range → data is more spread out  |  small range → data is more consistent

Quick example
3791115
Range = 12
Highest = 15
Lowest = 3
Range = 15 − 3 = 12
Step by Step

How to Find the Range

Worked Example

The ages of players on a football team:

17191622282118
1
Write out the data in order:
16, 17, 18, 19, 21, 22, 28
2
Identify the maximum value: 28
3
Identify the minimum value: 16
4
Calculate: Range = 28 − 16 = 12

⚠️ Watch Out!

✗  Don't confuse range with mean or median — they measure different things.

✓  Range is always a single positive number (or zero).

✗  You only need two values — highest and lowest.

Key vocabulary
Maximum  —  the largest value
Minimum  —  the smallest value
Spread  —  how far apart the values are
Consistent  —  similar values, small range

Interpretation

What does range tell us?

📏

Consistency

A small range means values are close together — the data is consistent.

Range = 3 → very consistent
📊

Variability

A large range means values are far apart — the data is more variable or unpredictable.

Range = 47 → high variability
⚖️

Comparison

Compare ranges across groups to judge which is more reliable or stable.

Smaller range = more reliable

✍️ Exam-style interpretation sentence

"The range for [group A] is [value], compared to [value] for [group B]. This means [group A]'s results are more consistent / less spread."

Real-World Context

Range in the Real World

🌡️

Weather

A city with a temperature range of 5°C is far more stable than one with a range of 40°C.

⚽

Sport

A goalkeeper who concedes 0–2 goals (range 2) is more consistent than one who concedes 0–8 (range 8).

💊

Medicine

A drug dosage with a range of 2mg is safer to administer than one with a range of 30mg.

📈

Finance

A stock price that varies by £2 is far more stable than one varying by £80.

🎓

Exam Results

Class A: Range = 12. Class B: Range = 48. Class A performed more consistently.

🏭

Manufacturing

A factory needs a small range in product dimensions to ensure quality control.

Range is the simplest measure of spread — always use it alongside the mean for a fuller picture.

Common Mistakes

Tackle the Misconceptions

Click each card to reveal the correction.

❌
"The range of 4, 8, 3, 10, 6 is 10."
✅ Incorrect! Range = Max − Min = 10 − 3 = 7. Range is the difference, not the maximum value.
❌
"A larger range always means better data."
✅ Incorrect! A larger range means more spread / variability. Whether that's "better" depends on context. Often, consistency (small range) is preferable.
❌
"You need to find the range of all values, not just two."
✅ Incorrect! Range only requires the maximum and minimum values. All other values in the dataset are ignored.
❌
"Range and mean tell you the same thing."
✅ Incorrect! Mean measures the average (central tendency). Range measures spread. Two datasets can share the same mean but have very different ranges.
